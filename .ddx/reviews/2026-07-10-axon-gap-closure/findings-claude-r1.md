### Verdict: REQUEST_CHANGES

### Summary

The plan is structurally sound, correctly files the four unfiled/under-covered core gaps and wires them into the final readiness bead `axon-b4f5bb82` (directly answering review question 6), and is appropriately verification-first for audit-atomicity. But it carries four BLOCKING defects that would cause incompatible implementations and a false readiness signal: the "reserved system collection" concept it promises to protect does not exist and the `__` handler exemption is a live public bypass; phase 4 demands batch/op limits that CONTRACT-001 never defines; the audit-atomicity invariant lacks a system-collection carve-out; and — most damaging — core beads (`axon-f48352d5`, `axon-36a3ce2b`, `axon-03269bc7`) are marked closed on the 0.4.0 target while the behavior is verifiably absent, so the final HELIX verdict would inherit an overclaiming tracker. Finish line A is achievable once these are resolved, but finish line B is not honestly measurable today because FEAT-032 has neither acceptance criteria nor a PRD success criterion to close against.
